Artisans of Rebirth

Across cultures and generations, craft has always been a way to connect, to create meaning, beauty, and livelihood. Anka brings together artisan producers from Türkiye and Syria, many of whom have been displaced or affected by the earthquake and ongoing conflict. By sharing their handmade goods with supportive communities in the UK, we foster collaboration, preserve heritage, and help build steady futures for those rebuilding their lives.

"We lost our factory, but not our hands. Anka gave us a way to keep pressing olive oil the way our mothers taught us. When people in the UK buy it, they taste our strength."

Zeynep - Zeytinkadın Kooperatifi, Hatay

"We paint tiles by hand, just like my grandfather did. After the war and quake, I thought our craft would disappear. Anka gave it a place to be seen again. A reason to keep painting."

Dima - Noor el-Fan, Damascus

"My family's spice blend zahter was almost forgotten after we lost our shop. Anka helped us label, package, and sell again."

Zehra – Zehra’nın Baharatları, Antakya

"After the earthquake, my kitchen was the only safe place left. Anka helped me turn it into work again. Now our erişte reaches UK shelves and I can support my daughter’s education."

Fatma - Erişte Hatunları, Malatya

"Embroidery helped me hold myself together. Through Anka, it became my work. Each shawl is stitched with memory and healing. I never imagined they’d be worn in cities I’ve never seen."

Rana – Atelier Suzan, Antakya

How you can help

Anka works by connecting local UK businesses like cafés, shops, and galleries with artisan producers in Türkiye and Syria. When you offer just a little shelf space, you help these makers share their craft with the world, earn a sustainable income, and rebuild their lives with dignity

We don’t replace their brands, we support them. Each product keeps its original label and identity. Anka simply helps carry it from one community to another.
